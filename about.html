About the Author

About the Author & This Book

My name is Bradley Hobbs, and this book began with a question that would not leave me alone.

For years I watched fear affect people in ways that seemed larger than the fears themselves. Fear influenced decisions, relationships, opportunities, and the way people viewed the future. The more I observed those patterns, the more I became convinced that fear was doing important work long before anyone consciously felt afraid.

That observation led to a simple question:

What if fear begins before we feel it?

The deeper I explored that question, the more I realized that fear is often misunderstood. Most people encounter fear at its loudest moments. They notice anxiety, worry, dread, overthinking, and emotional exhaustion. Those experiences are real, yet they are frequently the result of a process that started much earlier.

This book became an exploration of that hidden process.

Why I Wrote This Book

Fear Before Fear was written to help readers look beneath the surface of fear and discover where it begins. Rather than focusing only on fear after it becomes overwhelming, this book explores the stories, assumptions, expectations, and interpretations that often shape fear before fear is ever consciously experienced.

My hope was never simply to write a book about fear.

My hope was to help people understand it.

Understanding creates awareness.

Awareness creates clarity.

Clarity creates the opportunity for change.

Many fears become powerful because they remain unexplored. Once fear's origins become visible, fear often loses much of the mystery that allows it to grow unnoticed.

A Final Thought

Life will always contain uncertainty.

Questions will remain unanswered.

The future will continue unfolding one page at a time.

My hope is that this book has encouraged you to recognize when fear begins asking for authority it has not earned. My hope is that you leave these pages with a greater awareness of the stories you tell yourself, the assumptions you accept, and the possibilities you allow to shape your expectations.

Most importantly, my hope is that you remember that fear's predictions are not promises, fear's stories are not reality, and fear's assumptions are not facts.

The future remains unwritten.

Your story is still unfolding.

Thank you for taking this journey with me.

Grace and peace,

Bradley Hobbs
